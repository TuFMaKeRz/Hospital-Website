Patient Information

We want your experience at MedLife General Hospital to be as comfortable and stress-free as possible. Below you'll find important information to help you prepare for your visit or stay.

Before Your Visit

  • Registration

    Please arrive 30 minutes before your scheduled appointment to complete registration. Bring your insurance card, photo ID, and any relevant medical records.

  • What to Bring

    Current medications list, insurance information, comfortable clothing, personal toiletries, and any assistive devices you may need.

  • Preparing for Procedures

    Follow all pre-procedure instructions provided by your doctor, including fasting guidelines if applicable.

During Your Stay

  • Your Care Team

    You'll be cared for by a team of specialists including your attending physician, nurses, and support staff who will coordinate your treatment plan.

  • Patient Safety

    For your safety, please wear your ID bracelet at all times and inform staff of any allergies or special needs.

  • Patient Rights

    You have the right to be informed about your treatment, access your medical records, and make decisions about your care.

Billing & Insurance

Insurance Information

We accept most major insurance plans. Please verify with your insurance provider that our hospital and physicians are in-network prior to your visit.

Our billing department can assist with insurance questions and claims. Contact them at (800) 555-6789 or billing@medlifehospital.com.

Financial Assistance

We offer financial assistance programs for qualifying patients. Applications are available at our patient services desk or can be downloaded from our website.

Visitor Information

We welcome visitors and recognize their importance in patient recovery. Please review our visitor guidelines before your visit.

Visiting Hours

  • General Wards

    10:00 AM - 8:00 PM daily

  • Intensive Care Units

    11:00 AM - 12:00 PM and 5:00 PM - 6:00 PM

  • Maternity Ward

    9:00 AM - 9:00 PM (Partners may stay overnight)

  • Pediatrics

    Parents may stay 24 hours; other visitors 10:00 AM - 8:00 PM

Visitor Guidelines

  • Health & Safety

    Visitors with colds, flu, or other contagious illnesses should not visit patients.

  • Number of Visitors

    Generally 2 visitors per patient at a time, with exceptions for special circumstances.

  • Children Visitors

    Children under 12 must be supervised at all times and may require special permission in certain units.

  • Overnight Stays

    Only permitted in private rooms and must be arranged in advance with nursing staff.

Parking & Directions

Parking Information

Visitor parking is available in the garage adjacent to the main hospital entrance. The first 30 minutes are free, with discounted rates for patients and visitors.

Valet parking is available at the main entrance from 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM for $10.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get copies of my medical records?

You can request copies of your medical records by completing an Authorization for Release of Health Information form, available at our medical records department or on our website. Please allow 7-10 business days for processing.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept cash, personal checks, money orders, and all major credit cards. Payment plans may be available for qualifying patients.

Can I choose my own doctor?

Yes, you may choose your attending physician if they have admitting privileges at our hospital. If you don't have a doctor, we can help you select one from our medical staff.

How do I provide feedback about my experience?

We welcome your feedback. You may complete a patient satisfaction survey after discharge, speak with our patient relations representative, or submit comments through our website.

What are my rights as a patient?

Patients have the right to considerate and respectful care, complete information about their diagnosis and treatment, participate in care decisions, privacy and confidentiality, and to voice grievances. A complete list of patient rights is provided upon admission.
